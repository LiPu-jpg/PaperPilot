# Common Academic Phrases

Transition phrases organized by function.

## Introducing

- "We propose a novel..."
- "This paper introduces..."
- "Our approach is based on..."
- "The key idea is to..."

## Background

- "Recent work has shown..."
- "Prior research demonstrates..."
- "A common approach is..."
- "Traditionally, this problem is solved by..."

## Contrasting

- "However, these methods suffer from..."
- "In contrast, our approach..."
- "Unlike previous work, we..."
- "While X achieves good results, it fails to..."

## Adding/Building

- "Furthermore, we also..."
- "In addition to X, we..."
- "Building on this, we..."
- "Beyond that, we..."

## Explaining Method

- "Specifically, we..."
- "More precisely..."
- "To achieve this, we..."
- "The main component is..."

## Results

- "Experimental results show..."
- "We observe that..."
- "As shown in Table..."
- "Our method achieves..."

## Qualifying

- "It is worth noting that..."
- "One limitation is..."
- "We acknowledge that..."
- "While promising, this approach..."

## Concluding

- "In summary, we have..."
- "To conclude, we..."
- "These results demonstrate..."
- "This work provides..."

## Citations

- "According to Smith et al. (2020)..."
- "Previous studies (Jones, 2019) have shown..."
- "As demonstrated in [12]..."
- "See also [5, 6] for..."

## Figures/Tables

- "As illustrated in Figure 1..."
- "Table 2 shows that..."
- "As can be seen from..."
- "The dashed line in Figure 3 represents..."

## Statistical

- "with a statistically significant improvement (p<0.05)"
- "on average"
- "with standard deviation"
- "across all datasets"
